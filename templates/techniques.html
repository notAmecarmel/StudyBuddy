Effective Study Techniques

One click to Study Smarter!

1. Pomodoro Technique

What it is: Breaks study sessions into 25-minute intervals with short breaks in between.

  • Work for 25 minutes.
  • Take a 5-minute break.
  • After 4 cycles, take a 15-30 minute break.
Use Now

2. Active Recall

What it is: Actively test yourself instead of passively reading notes.

  • Read a section and recall the details without notes.
  • Write or say the information aloud.
  • Identify gaps and review weak areas.
Use Now

3. Spaced Repetition

What it is: Review material at increasing intervals to improve retention.

  • Use flashcards or apps like Anki.
  • Review difficult topics more frequently.
  • Space out sessions over days or weeks.
Use Now

4. Feynman Technique

What it is: Simplify a topic and explain it in basic terms.

  • Write the concept on a blank page.
  • Explain it as if teaching a child.
  • Refine and clarify weak areas.

5. Interleaving

What it is: Alternate between topics to improve understanding and retention.

  • Study multiple subjects in one session.
  • Spend short intervals on each topic.
  • Rotate subjects to reinforce learning.

6. Mind Mapping

What it is: Organize information visually using a central topic and branches.

  • Create a central topic.
  • Draw branches for subtopics.
  • Use colors and images for better recall.
